What to Take Camping

Must Haves

  • Tent: A good rule of thumb is to aim for a tent that sleeps 1 or 2 people more than you need, for space for all your camping essentials!

  • Sleeping Bags: Offering the perfect night's sleep in the outdoors!, sleeping bags offer warmth and comfort for all-season camping.

  • Camping Stoves: A versatile method for cooking meals and heating water in the outdoors. Don't forget to take the correct type of fuel for your camping stove model!

  • Cooking Pans & Utensils: A common camping cooking mistake is forgetting to bring camping cooking equipment, bring your cooking pans & utensils for a variety of excellent outdoor meals around the campfire.

  • Torches & Lanterns: For lighting the tent in the evening, and any night-time trips to the loo (don't forget extra batteries or you can carry solar ones).

  • Water Carrier: So you don't have to go to the site tap every time you need water.

  • Clothing: When it comes to camping, having appropriate clothing is not just about showing off expensive gear or fashionable apparel. It’s primarily about ensuring your comfort, protection, and safety in various weather conditions, temperatures, and terrains. Camping clothes are about functionality first.

Health and Safety:

  • Personal Medicines: It's important to take your medicines and also some general medicines(cold, fever, vomit).

  • Travel First Aid Kit: For minor injuries and ailments (e.g Anti-Bacterial Handwash).

  • Toilet Rolls: For all types of emergencies!

Quick and useful suggestions
to help you

  • Plan Ahead: Research your campsite, check maps, and learn about local rules. It saves you from last-minute stress or getting lost

  • Check the weather: Pack according to the forecast. A raincoat or sunblock can make or break your trip.

  • Set up camp early: Arrive before sunset so you have time to pitch your tent, organize your gear, and settle in safely.

  • Stay hydrated and eat well Drink enough water and bring easy-to-cook meals. Energy bars and fruits are great for quick snacks.

  • Respect nature: Don’t litter, avoid loud music, and leave the site as clean as you found it.

  • Be safe with fire: Keep a bucket of water nearby and never leave your campfire unattended.
